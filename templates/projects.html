Botball

 

Botball is a worldwide autonomous robotics competition for high school students. Every year, a new game board is released, and teams are challenged with building and programming robots to complete tasks on that board without any human interaction or intervention. In 2013 I was elected captain of Hampton Botball, and I remained in that position until I graduated in 2016. During that time, I lead the team to three global top 10 finishes and a world championship victory. Check out the videos below for a taste of what exactly a botball robot looks like, and follow the link to github for the PI Motor Control and Line Following libraries I wrote for our bots!

Touch+

 

Touch+ is a stylish wearable that utilizes stereo vision and haptic feedback to assist the blind in navitgation. Touch+ was first developed at the Spring 2016 Johns Hopkins Hackathon, where it won 3rd place overall. After the hackathon, our team was approached by a couple of venture capitalists who wanted to give us a chance to pitch out idea to them. After spending countless hours rehersing our pitch and revising our slide deck, we took the dive and ended up securing $10,000 in funding from JH Tech Ventures and $1,000 in fuding from A-Level Capital. We have since been working alongside the National Federation for the Blind to create our beta prototype.
